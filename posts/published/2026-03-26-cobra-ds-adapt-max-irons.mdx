MAX is not a casual label in Cobra's naming scheme. It sits at the top of their forgiveness range — wider sole than the standard DS-ADAPT, taller face, more offset at the hosel. The face flexes on off-center strikes because of a thin, hot-face construction, which means the gap between your best shot and your worst shot narrows. You will not feel the blade-like feedback that some golfers chase, because this club is not designed to give you feedback on mistakes. It is designed to absorb them.

The hollow body does two things: it lowers the center of gravity, and it lets the face move more freely on impact. The practical result is that shots from the heel or the toe still travel with reasonable speed and reasonable direction. Not as good as a center strike. But you are not writing off the hole the moment you catch it slightly thin.

The rails on the sole — Cobra carries their Baffler Rail technology into this iron — help on shots from rough and on tight lies. Most amateur golfers underestimate how much sole design affects turf interaction. A wide, railed sole digs less and skims more. The clubhead does not bury on a slightly heavy strike. The shot comes out. Sometimes it comes out better than it deserves to.
